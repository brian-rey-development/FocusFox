# Interview Title

## Interview context

**Goal:** What we want to learn from this interview.

**Customer segment:** Who we are talking to and their relationship to the product.

**Stage:** discovery | validation | post-launch

**Hypotheses being tested:**
- Hypothesis 1
- Hypothesis 2

## Ground rules

Reminders for the interviewer before starting:

- Talk about their life, not your idea
- Ask about past behavior, not future intent ("Would you...?" is a red flag)
- No pitching - if you catch yourself explaining the product, stop
- Every opinion is worthless; every story is gold
- Silence is okay - let them fill it
- You are here to be wrong, not to be validated

## Opening script

Use this to set the tone without priming the interviewee.

> "Thanks for making time. I am working on understanding [problem space] better. I am not here to pitch anything - I just want to hear about your experience. There are no right or wrong answers. Is it okay if I take notes?"

## Wrap-up questions

Always close with these. Do not skip them.

- "Is there anything I should have asked but did not?"
- "Who else do you think I should talk to about this?"
- "Would it be okay if I followed up with you again as this evolves?"
